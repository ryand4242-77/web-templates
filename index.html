How it's made

The Davidson Standard

Every piece that leaves the shop is held to four promises. They're the reason a Davidson table outlives the people who buy it.

i

Sourced honestly

We work in locally felled Appalachian hardwoods — white oak, walnut, cherry, and maple — air-dried and milled in our own shop. We know which ridge each board came off.

ii

Joined to last

Mortise-and-tenon frames and hand-cut dovetails do the structural work — not screws or staples. Joinery is cut to fit by hand, the way it's held for centuries.

iii

Finished by hand

Surfaces are hand-planed and sanded through the grits, then sealed with oil-and-wax finishes that deepen with age and can be renewed at home — never sprayed plastic.

iv

Built for generations

Every piece is signed, dated, and guaranteed for the life of the joinery. We build furniture meant to be handed down, not replaced.

“If it won't last a hundred years, it doesn't leave the shop.” — The Davidson Standard

Our Story

A small shop in the
Blue Ridge.

Davidson & Co. is a family woodworking shop tucked into the foothills of the Blue Ridge Mountains. What began as a single workbench in a backyard garage has grown into a working studio — but the philosophy hasn't moved an inch: build honest furniture, by hand, from wood we can stand behind.

We take on a limited number of commissions each season so that every piece gets the time it deserves. When you order from us, you're talking to the person holding the chisel — not a catalog.
